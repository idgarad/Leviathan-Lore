# CX-010: Science, Survey, and Recovery Operations

Document Status: Draft
Primary Layer: Modern Times
Subject Domain: Technology
Audience: Developers | Authors | Readers

## Abstract

This volume defines how the modern empire encounters the unknown at operational scale. It describes orbital surveys, away missions, anomaly scans, extended expeditions, evidence collection, contamination protocols, distress beacons, and Imperial recovery procedures. It is the practical doctrine through which modern institutions convert dangerous discovery into usable knowledge without pretending discovery is safe.

## Diegetic Record

The empire does not learn the frontier by proclamation. It learns it by sending crews into places where records fail. Survey science, archaeological recovery, orbital telemetry, and anomaly response are therefore among the most consequential disciplines of the modern age. The map is not simply expanded. It is interrogated.

Most first contact with unknown territory occurs through orbital survey. Bodies are mapped, signatures cataloged, structures outlined, and risk estimated before a hull is cut open or a boot touches regolith. This is the lowest violence form of inquiry available to the frontier, and the empire prefers it wherever possible. It is cheaper, cleaner, and less likely to awaken whatever should have remained undisturbed.

When orbit is not enough, away missions follow. These are small expeditionary detachments built around scientific, engineering, medical, security, and specialist capacity. Their work is bounded and specific: recover samples, map ruins, breach sealed compartments, extract archives, confirm contamination, or stabilize derelict infrastructure long enough to learn what it is. Wardens may authorize the mission and secure the theater, but the actual labor of encounter belongs to trained field teams.

Anomaly scanning occupies the threshold between science and taboo. It is used where ordinary survey reveals timing drift, ghost contacts, hostile geometry, unexplained signals, or other symptoms of misalignment. Probe ladders, remote instruments, and carefully escalated exposure are preferred because the empire has learned that knowledge gathered too quickly often arrives malformed.

The danger is not only environmental. Crews may return contaminated biologically, cognitively, or memetically. Data may be altered by the act of acquisition. Artifacts may require vault handling rather than ordinary cargo processing. Abandonment under such conditions is one of the gravest crimes of frontier command. Distress beacons, local pickup attempts, and Imperial Recovery Services exist because the empire knows the frontier produces situations in which competence and catastrophe are separated by minutes.

Thus science in Leviathan is not detached curiosity. It is disciplined trespass into a universe that has repeatedly demonstrated its capacity to erase, mislead, infect, or simply outlast the observer.

## Analytical Breakdown

### Layer Function

- Modern Times: This is a primary modern-layer operations document. It defines how current institutions gather evidence and survive contact with old ruins, anomalies, and frontier unknowns.
- Ancient History: Many missions target materially recoverable sites whose contexts are incomplete but not wholly unknowable.
- Hyborean Era: Mythic remnants, heroic ruins, and half-understood legacies often require specialist recovery rather than simple excavation.
- Eldritch Layer: This layer becomes operationally relevant when misalignment, memetic hazard, or ontological wrongness exceeds routine scientific handling.

### Origins

Formal survey and recovery doctrine emerged because imperial expansion is fundamentally a process of rediscovery. The empire required repeatable methods for dealing with ruins, derelicts, signals, and contaminated evidence without reducing every encounter to brute-force salvage.

### Development

The operational system matured into several distinct mission classes.

- Orbital survey maps, classifies, and risk-screens a target before crew exposure.
- Away missions deploy small specialist detachments for surface or interior objectives.
- Anomaly scans use staged probes and controlled escalation to study dangerous irregularities.
- Extended expeditions support long-duration work where staging, resupply, and containment matter as much as discovery.
- Recovery doctrine formalizes distress beacons, rescue attempts, and Imperial intervention when crews are stranded or contaminated.

### Present Understanding

The modern exploratory order rests on several shared assumptions.

- Specialist squads, not bridge crews, perform most direct fieldwork.
- Evidence is graded not only by value, but by hazard, contamination risk, and institutional sensitivity.
- Artifacts and data often require secure handling through vault or controlled container systems rather than open transport.
- Abandonment of deployed crews is both a legal offense and a reputational catastrophe.
- Recovery may succeed, fail, expose a faction politically, or trigger quarantine even when the crew survives.

## Material Implications

- Exploration becomes a major source of prestige, scandal, and political leverage rather than a simple neutral profession.
- The empire's fear of contaminated evidence becomes more believable when survey doctrine is shown to include quarantine, staged probing, and recovery law.
- Frontier operations support stories about professional competence under pressure, not just heroic improvisation.
- Scientific discovery remains valuable, but it is always entangled with legal exposure, institutional secrecy, and cult attention.
- This doctrine allows the setting to treat archaeology, ruin exploration, and anomaly response as structurally important rather than as side activity.

## Author Notes

Purpose: This volume operationalizes how modern Leviathan civilization investigates the unknown.

Reasoning: The setting already has campaigns, ruins, artifacts, and dangerous evidence. It needed a codex explaining how institutions actually approach those encounters so discovery feels procedural, expensive, and risky rather than casual.

Layer Function Checkpoints:
- Modern Times: Primary location of institutional method, rescue law, contamination handling, and archive production.
- Ancient History: Supplies the recoverable targets that make the doctrine necessary.
- Hyborean Era: Supplies the interpretive ambiguity and relic prestige that complicate survey work.
- Eldritch Layer: Supplies the threshold where evidence becomes hazardous to the observer and ordinary method begins to fail.

Math And Constraint Checks: If away missions routinely deploy specialist teams, later volumes need consistent assumptions about crew availability, casualty replacement, evacuation windows, and mission duration. If distress recovery is a serious Imperial service, later volumes need credible timelines, costs, and failure rates. If contamination spreads through crews or systems, later volumes need clear thresholds and quarantine consequences.

FTL Hand-Waves: This volume intentionally avoids defining transit physics. Where extended expeditions require long-distance staging, that requirement should be read as logistical reality rather than as a statement about any specific FTL mechanism.

Open Questions: What classes of anomaly require immediate ISC override; how common are formal recovery interventions; what legal thresholds turn contamination into a mandatory quarantine event; how much classified evidence can a House suppress before the empire intervenes.
