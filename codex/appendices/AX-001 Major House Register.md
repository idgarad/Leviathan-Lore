# AX-001: Major House Register

Document Status: Draft Appendix
Primary Layer: Modern Times
Subject Domain: Faction
Audience: Developers | Authors | Readers

## Abstract

This appendix records the current public register of the Imperial Family and the twenty-three major houses. It is not a full dynastic history. It is a working reference to the house hierarchy, broad governing styles, and the institutional identities by which these houses are commonly understood.

## Diegetic Record

The empire speaks often of Houses in the abstract, but abstractions do not levy worlds, bargain over charters, or preserve grudges across campaigns. The following register records the sovereign apex of the modern Imperial feudal order as it is publicly described.

## Analytical Breakdown

### Registry Notes

- The Imperial Family stands above all major houses.
- Twenty-three major houses hold ancestral standing beneath the Throne.
- Each major house has a public governing style, a seat-star tradition, and a bannerman network of lesser houses.
- Homeworld sector assignments beyond the Throne itself remain subject to formal registry and campaign outcome where not yet explicitly fixed in published canon.

## Material Implications

- The house system becomes more legible once the abstract major houses are attached to public institutional identities.
- Rivalry, cooperation, and House specialization can be grounded in stable register entries rather than improvised from case to case.
- The register supports later faction, sector, and campaign lore without forcing immediate full dynastic histories for every house.

## Author Notes

Purpose: This appendix gives the major-house layer of the empire a stable public-facing roster.

Reasoning: The governance volumes define the structure, but downstream lore also needs a manageable registry of who the major players are and how they are generally understood.

Layer Function Checkpoints:
- Modern Times: Primary function as the active political registry of the living feudal order.
- Ancient History: Major-house identity often rests on claims about long memory, old service, and inherited legitimacy.
- Hyborean Era: Some houses cultivate mythic depth in their self-presentation even when the registry itself is modern.
- Eldritch Layer: No direct registry function, but old ruins, archives, and absences often shape House ideology and strategic posture.

Math And Constraint Checks: If twenty-three major houses meaningfully steward large regions of the empire, later volumes need realistic assumptions about sector counts, bannerman distribution, staff density, and fleet burden. If these public identities matter politically, later volumes should preserve coherent specialization instead of making every house equally generic.

FTL Hand-Waves: None.

Open Questions: Which rivalries are most canonically load-bearing beyond Keitel-Veyra and Bakken-Dremer; which houses are oldest by reliable record rather than self-claim; how many sectors each major house directly stewards at the present point in canon.
