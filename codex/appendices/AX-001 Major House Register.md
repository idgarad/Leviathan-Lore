# AX-001: Major House Register

Document Status: Draft Appendix
Primary Layer: Modern Times
Subject Domain: Faction
Audience: Developers | Authors | Readers

## Abstract

This appendix records the current public register of the Imperial Family and the twenty-three major houses. It is not a full dynastic history. It is a working reference to the house hierarchy, broad governing styles, and the institutional identities by which these houses are commonly understood.

## Diegetic Record

The empire speaks often of Houses in the abstract, but abstractions do not levy worlds, bargain over charters, or preserve grudges across campaigns. The following register records the sovereign apex of the modern Imperial feudal order as it is publicly described.

## Analytical Breakdown

### Registry Notes

- The Imperial Family stands above all major houses.
- Twenty-three major houses hold ancestral standing beneath the Throne.
- Each major house has a public governing style, a seat-star tradition, and a bannerman network of lesser houses.
- Homeworld sector assignments beyond the Throne itself remain subject to formal registry and campaign outcome where not yet explicitly fixed in published canon.

### Registered Houses

- The Imperial Throne: Imperial seat at Imperius; supreme sovereign authority; ancient lineage and absolute sovereignty.
- House Keitel: Seat-star Silanthos; espionage style; calculating, patient, and well-networked; survives through intelligence and political timing.
- House Veyra: Seat-star Veranthos; military style; aggressive, proud, and martial; feared for direct fleet action.
- House Drake: Seat-star Drakonis; expansionist style; bold, ambitious, and frontier-hardened; noted for exceptional territorial conquest.
- House Bakken: Seat-star Pallanthos; diplomatic style; pragmatic, resourceful, and politically agile; known for coalition-building and dividend leverage.
- House Dremer: Seat-star Furnaxis; industrial style; efficient, methodical, infrastructure-focused; turns frontier claims into productive fiefdoms quickly.
- House Veynar: Seat-star Auranthos; mercantile style; wealthy, commerce-driven, and connected through vault and trade networks.
- House Solenne: Seat-star Solennar; scholastic style; scholarly, cautious, and archive-minded; treats knowledge as political currency.
- House Malachar: Seat-star Malakhor; military style; brutal, honor-bound siege specialists with a reputation for decisive long campaigns.
- House Tarveyn: Seat-star Tarventhos; diplomatic style; conciliatory, long-memory, mediation-focused; trusted by few and needed by many.
- House Orenthal: Seat-star Orenthos; industrial style; manufacturing-focused, guild-connected, and reliable; backbone of major planetary industry.
- House Cressida: Seat-star Vespertis; espionage style; secretive, patient, dynastic planners working on generational timelines.
- House Valdris: Seat-star Valdaron; military style; disciplined, fleet-heavy, strategic; maintains one of the largest non-Imperial fleets.
- House Orhaven: Seat-star Orventhos; agricultural style; patient colony-builders able to turn frontier worlds into breadbaskets quickly.
- House Tanneck: Seat-star Ferranthos; mining style; resource-focused and hardy; dominates asteroid and deep-space extraction.
- House Solvar: Seat-star Solvarion; mercantile style; pragmatic trade-network operators controlling vital corridors and toll routes.
- House Meridin: Seat-star Meridax; diplomatic style; honorable, treaty-keeping, steadfast; treats oath as serious political capital.
- House Caelorn: Seat-star Caelestis; scholastic-navigational style; navigators, void-explorers, and map-keepers with disproportionate route leverage.
- House Duskmore: Seat-star Umbraxis; espionage style; shadow political operators, debt collectors, and keepers of long grudges.
- House Ashvane: Seat-star Pyranthos; expansionist style; aggressive colonizers and risk-tolerant frontier opportunists.
- House Pellery: Seat-star Pellarion; industrial style; shipwrights and orbital constructors producing major fractions of Imperial tonnage.
- House Mourne: Seat-star Bastior; military style; fortress-builders and stubborn defenders specializing in attritional survival.
- House Verath: Seat-star Verathos; mercantile-financial style; financiers and contract brokers dominating Imperial share and dividend markets.
- House Stralken: Seat-star Stralthar; agricultural-traditional style; loyal, old-feudal, and clan-oriented; slow to adapt and exceptionally reliable.

## Material Implications

- The house system becomes more legible once the abstract major houses are attached to public institutional identities.
- Rivalry, cooperation, and House specialization can be grounded in stable register entries rather than improvised from case to case.
- The register supports later faction, sector, and campaign lore without forcing immediate full dynastic histories for every house.

## Author Notes

Purpose: This appendix gives the major-house layer of the empire a stable public-facing roster.

Reasoning: The governance volumes define the structure, but downstream lore also needs a manageable registry of who the major players are and how they are generally understood.

Layer Function Checkpoints:
- Modern Times: Primary function as the active political registry of the living feudal order.
- Ancient History: Major-house identity often rests on claims about long memory, old service, and inherited legitimacy.
- Hyborean Era: Some houses cultivate mythic depth in their self-presentation even when the registry itself is modern.
- Eldritch Layer: No direct registry function, but old ruins, archives, and absences often shape House ideology and strategic posture.

Math And Constraint Checks: If twenty-three major houses meaningfully steward large regions of the empire, later volumes need realistic assumptions about sector counts, bannerman distribution, staff density, and fleet burden. If these public identities matter politically, later volumes should preserve coherent specialization instead of making every house equally generic.

FTL Hand-Waves: None.

Open Questions: Which rivalries are most canonically load-bearing beyond Keitel-Veyra and Bakken-Dremer; which houses are oldest by reliable record rather than self-claim; how many sectors each major house directly stewards at the present point in canon.
