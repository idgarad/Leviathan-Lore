# AX-003: Territorial Survey Hierarchy and the Canonical T0 Map

Document Status: Draft
Primary Layer: Modern Times
Subject Domain: Cartography
Audience: Developers | Authors | Readers

## Abstract

This appendix fixes the operational cartography used by the Imperial Survey Corps. It defines the hierarchy of territory, region, survey sector, and system; records the gate classes attached to each layer; and establishes T0 as the canonical Imperial Throne territory map used as a technical reference in later lore.

## Diegetic Record

The ISC does not wait for poetry. Before a frontier can be romanticized, inherited, or sanctified, it must be made legible. A campaign territory is therefore reduced first into a survey lattice that can be audited, supplied, contested, and named in stable sequence.

In current survey doctrine, every opened territory is bounded as a single campaign volume. That volume is partitioned into regions, each region into survey sectors, and each survey sector into addressed systems. The older constitutional language of sectors still survives in Imperial law, where a sector may mean a broad enduring district of rule. Survey officers therefore speak more precisely when working inside a territory, because their chain of custody runs territory to region to sector to system.

In frontier grant practice, the survey sector is the fundamental fiefdom. A major house may be awarded stewardship over a sector as a coherent logistical and political unit, then assign governors, lease-holders, or subordinate houses to the individual systems inside it. The empire prefers this scale because a single system is too narrow to support meaningful frontier obligation, while a full region is too large to hand out casually during active campaign conditions.

This hierarchy is not decorative. Heavy gates bind regions to one another. Medium gates bind sectors within a region. Light gates bind local systems within a sector. Freight, fleet response, casualty evacuation, and legal recordkeeping all inherit that same structure. The survey lattice becomes the practical skeleton of the campaign long before later dynastic, provincial, or ceremonial names settle over it.

The Throne's canonical territory map is held under designator T0. In its current authoritative survey pass, T0 spans roughly 130 by 130 by 65 light-years, resolves into sixteen primary regions, and catalogs seven thousand viable systems under a four-light-year minimum separation standard. Its regions are still routinely cited by technical designators R00 through R15 in survey and traffic archives even where court, civic, or house naming traditions overlay them.

System notation preserves that chain. A Throne survey address such as T0R07S03-SYS12 states the territory, region, survey sector, and indexed system in that order. The notation survives because the empire cannot afford to lose archival continuity every time honorific geography changes.

## Analytical Breakdown

### Layer Function

- Modern Times: This appendix defines the present operational grammar used by the empire to map, move through, and administer reopened space.
- Ancient History: The survey lattice exists partly because rediscovered space is full of older political scars and ruined jurisdictions that require stable technical reference.
- Hyborean Era: Survey notation permits modern institutions to catalog legendary or disputed sites before later interpretation settles around them.
- Eldritch Layer: Stable addressing gives the setting a repeatable way to track hostile or contaminating discoveries without relying on local naming traditions.

### Territorial Stack

- Territory: The bounded campaign volume opened for rediscovery, contest, and settlement.
- Region: The first-order survey cell inside a territory. Heavy-gate routing and high-level territorial structure operate at this scale.
- Survey Sector: The second-order survey cell inside a region. Medium-gate anchors and local logistical clustering operate at this scale.
- System: The individual addressed star system inside a survey sector. Local light-gate traffic operates at this scale.

### Grant Scale

- Survey sector stewardship is the default frontier fiefdom grant.
- System governorships are subordinate appointments made within a sector-scale grant.
- Regions matter operationally, but they are normally too large to function as routine first-pass noble grants during campaign play.

### Canonical T0 Baseline

- T0 is the canonical Imperial Throne territory map.
- The present T0 pass uses sixteen primary regions.
- The present T0 pass catalogs seven thousand viable systems.
- Region designators R00 through R15 remain authoritative technical references even when ceremonial naming overlays them.

### Transit Grammar

- Heavy gates are inter-region links.
- Medium gates are inter-sector links within a region.
- Light gates are intra-sector links that thicken the local system network.

## Material Implications

- Cartography, logistics, and sovereignty claims share the same territorial grammar.
- Sector stewardship gives campaign politics a meaningful tactical unit larger than a single world but smaller than a whole region.
- Courtly renaming can accumulate over time without breaking the archive because survey notation remains stable beneath it.
- T0 provides a fixed technical reference for later Throne-world, house-seat, and constitutional geography.
- Authors and developers can locate a site in the lattice before deciding what later local culture calls it.

## Author Notes

Purpose: This appendix locks down the spatial grammar implied by the GalaxySim territory generator so later lore, maps, and technical content refer to the same hierarchy.

Reasoning: CX-006 already explained campaign territories in broad political terms, but it still used sector language too loosely. The generator provides a stricter and more useful distinction between constitutional sectors and survey sectors, and T0 gives the repository a canonical throne-world reference case.

Layer Function Checkpoints:
- Modern Times: Fixes the current survey grammar of imperial expansion.
- Ancient History: Supports stable indexing of ruins, claims, and recovered jurisdictions.
- Hyborean Era: Lets legendary or partially understood sites be cataloged before interpreted.
- Eldritch Layer: Provides a repeatable archival frame for anomalous or contaminating discoveries.

Math And Constraint Checks: The current T0 reference pass uses a fixed span of 130 x 130 x 65 light-years, a seven-thousand-system count, a four-light-year minimum star separation standard, sixteen regions, and four to nine survey sectors per region. Later logistics, freight times, fleet doctrine, and settlement densities should respect that internal geometry and the heavy-medium-light gate hierarchy built on top of it.

FTL Hand-Waves: This appendix uses heavy, medium, and light gate classes as structural facts in line with CX-013's CHJ baseline, but it does not attempt to define detailed gate physics, exact throughput envelopes, or advanced-gate doctrine.

Open Questions: Which T0 regions are most often called by ceremonial rather than survey names in common speech; whether all campaign territories use the same sixteen-region survey frame or whether T0 is the Throne reference case; how often sector stewardships are subdivided among lesser houses versus kept under direct major-house governorship.
