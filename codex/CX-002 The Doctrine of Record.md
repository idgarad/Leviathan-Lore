# CX-002: The Doctrine of Record

Document Status: Foundational Canon
Primary Layer: Modern Times
Subject Domain: Doctrine
Audience: Developers | Authors | Readers

## Abstract

This volume defines how the Leviathan setting grades evidence and handles contradiction. It exists so the codex can contain myth, propaganda, fragmentary archaeology, and hard documentation without treating them as interchangeable.

## Diegetic Record

The modern historical discipline holds that all claims about the past are not born equal. Some are attested by converging records. Some are reconstructions from damaged but credible remnants. Some survive only as partisan testimony. Some endure as myth attached to material fact. Some arrive from depths so alien that their very legibility is suspect.

Accordingly, the schools of record commonly sort claims into five broad classes.

Verified Record consists of events or conditions supported by strong, independent, and mutually testing evidence. Administrative archives, material remains, sensor logs, accounting chains, and corroborated testimony all belong here.

Corroborated Reconstruction consists of claims that cannot be observed directly in full but can be inferred with confidence from surviving fragments. Missing years, collapsed archives, shattered fleet registries, and partial migration records often enter the canon by reconstruction.

Partisan Account consists of records that are internally valuable yet openly biased. Court chronicles, victory inscriptions, confessional documents, factional memoirs, and ideological histories belong in this class. They are not discarded, but neither are they trusted alone.

Mythic Memory consists of narratives that preserve genuine inheritance without preserving explanatory accuracy. Hero cycles, saint legends, oath-poems, taboo geographies, apocalyptic liturgies, and folk genealogies often carry distorted truths from the Hyborean or early Ancient layers.

Contaminated Contact is reserved for evidence marked by the Eldritch Layer: records that may be real, but are warped by encounter, cognitive damage, impossible temporal structure, non-anthropic signaling, or phenomena that compromise the observer. Such evidence is quarantined, not ignored.

This doctrine does not abolish uncertainty. It disciplines it. Contradiction becomes legible when each claim is first forced to declare what kind of witness it is.

## Analytical Breakdown

### Why Classification Matters

Without evidentiary classes, every contradiction becomes either a canon error or an excuse for arbitrariness. Classification allows the setting to contain disagreement while still having standards.

### Relationship To The Historical Layers

Modern Times should be dominated by Verified Record and Corroborated Reconstruction. Ancient History mixes those with more frequent Partisan Accounts. The Hyborean Era leans heavily on Mythic Memory anchored by material remains. The Eldritch Layer often appears through Contaminated Contact and second-order consequences.

### Use In Future Volumes

Later codex books can cite the evidentiary class of a claim when tone, certainty, or contradiction matters. This allows a clean distinction between what is definitely true, what is probably true, and what is merely inherited.

## Material Implications

The doctrine of record gives the franchise a way to support both mystery and rigor. Writers can include legends without flattening standards. Developers can identify what counts as hard canon for mechanics, mapmaking, and chronology. Readers can understand whether a shocking statement reflects fact, rumor, doctrine, or contamination.

It also supports refactoring. As new volumes are added, claims can be moved between evidentiary classes rather than simply overwritten. A Hyborean legend may later be elevated into corroborated reconstruction if enough artifacts are found. A trusted archive may be downgraded if it is exposed as a forgery.

## Author Notes

Purpose: This document provides the canon-management tool that keeps the setting flexible without becoming incoherent.

Reasoning: A franchise-scale setting needs a way to carry myths, lies, fragmentary records, and horror contamination at the same time. Evidentiary classes let contradictions exist in a controlled way.

Math And Constraint Checks: Claims can be uncertain, but consequences cannot be arbitrary. Even legends should tie back to artifacts, migrations, ecological change, ruins, or institutional memory if they matter materially.

FTL Hand-Waves: None in this volume.

Open Questions: Future volumes may add more granular subcategories if the archive becomes large enough to justify them.
