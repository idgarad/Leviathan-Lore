# CX-014: Power Generation

In the Leviathan setting, all shipboard and station power is provided by Compact Dimensional Acceleration Packs, or **CDAP units**.

CDAP units are advanced, self-contained power generation modules that exploit dimensional acceleration phenomena to produce vast amounts of usable energy in a compact form factor. They are the universal standard for power generation across all modern imperial technology, replacing older fusion, fission, and antimatter systems.

## CDAP Unit Operation

CDAP units operate by accelerating a minuscule quantity of xM—on the order of single atoms—within a compact particle accelerator core. Once initiated, the xM is forced to repeatedly jump in and out of CHJ space, producing two primary effects:

1. **Induced Electrical Current:** The dimensional transitions of the xM induce a strong electrical current, which is harvested as usable power for ship and station systems.
2. **Heat Generation:** Each transition also generates heat as a byproduct, which must be managed by the unit's integrated cooling system.

CDAP units are inherently self-regulating: as the unit heats up, its efficiency drops, causing output to decay gradually. If the unit reaches a critical thermal threshold, its output collapses entirely, and it ceases to function until cooled and reset. This behavior is analogous to a battery's discharge curve, with a slow decay followed by a sharp cutoff.

## CDAP Equilibrium and Shutdown Behavior

During normal operation, a CDAP unit reaches a natural equilibrium where its electrical output is balanced by the heat generated and removed by its cooling system. If the cooling system fails or is overwhelmed, the unit's temperature rises, causing output to decay. Upon reaching a critical temperature, the CDAP automatically shuts down to prevent damage.

Restarting a CDAP after shutdown is a nontrivial process: the initial acceleration and dimensional cycling of xM requires a tremendous surge of power, far beyond what a typical shipboard system can provide. As a result, jump-starting a CDAP almost always requires external support—most commonly from a space station or dedicated jump-start facility equipped to deliver the necessary startup energy.
