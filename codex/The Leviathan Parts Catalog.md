# The Leviathan Parts Catalog

This catalog is the authoritative registry of all standardized ship components, modules, and assemblies used in Leviathan-class starships and their derivatives. It supersedes calibration ship references for all production, procurement, and certification purposes.

## Catalog Structure
- Each entry shall include:
  - Component Name
  - Classification (e.g., Hull, Engine, Thermal, Power, Weapon, Life Support, etc.)
  - Standard Sizes/Variants
  - Key Specifications (dimensions, mass, power, etc.)
  - Manufacturer(s) or License Holders
  - Description and Notes
  - Cross-references to relevant USO codes or technical appendices

## Purpose
This document is maintained to ensure consistency, safety, and interoperability across all Leviathan program vessels. All new ship designs must source components from this catalog unless a variance is approved by USO and the Imperial Survey Corps.

# Hulls by Ship Class and Frame

---

## Tiny Class
- **Typical Gross Tonnage:** 10–100 t
- **Estimated SCU Count:** 1–2 SCUs
- **Length Range:** 5–15 m
- **Notes:** Minimal crew or single-occupant; exempt from standard clearance requirements.

  - **Bantam:** Smallest practical FTL tug, tender, and yard-utility frame
  - **Cutter:** Short-service utility and patrol frame

---

## Small Class
- **Typical Gross Tonnage:** 100–1,000 t
- **Estimated SCU Count:** 2–6 SCUs
- **Length Range:** 15–40 m
- **Notes:** Supports basic crew amenities and modular upgrades.

  - **Scout:** Long-range light frame built around endurance and sensors
  - **Clipper:** Fast courier or packet frame, often civilian

---

## Medium Class
- **Typical Gross Tonnage:** 1,000–10,000 t
- **Estimated SCU Count:** 6–20 SCUs
- **Length Range:** 40–120 m
- **Notes:** Multi-role, supports extended missions and specialized bays.

  - **Corvette:** First compact warship frame with a dedicated engineering spine
  - **Transport:** General-service hull for passengers, tenders, or mixed cargo
  - **Freighter:** Broad-beam cargo frame, civilian or military support
  - **Frigate:** Escort frame with a full naval engineering deck

---

## Large Class
- **Typical Gross Tonnage:** 10,000–100,000 t
- **Estimated SCU Count:** 20–60 SCUs
- **Length Range:** 120–350 m
- **Notes:** Multiple decks, redundant systems, large crew complements.

  - **Destroyer:** First sustained fleet-combat frame
  - **Cruiser:** Long-service line frame for multirole heavy work
  - **Carrier:** Volume-dominant frame built around internal bays and service decks

---

## Huge Class
- **Typical Gross Tonnage:** 100,000–1,000,000 t
- **Estimated SCU Count:** 60–200 SCUs
- **Length Range:** 350–1,000 m
- **Notes:** Extensive compartmentalization, high redundancy, multiple engineering bays.

  - **Battlecruiser:** Fast heavy line frame that trades some density for reach
  - **Battleship:** Dense heavy line frame built around direct combat endurance
  - **Carrier:** Large fleet carrier or supercarrier
  - **Capital:** Umbrella for first-rank heavy frames once plant scale matters more than silhouette

---

## Titanic Class
- **Typical Gross Tonnage:** 1,000,000+ t
- **Estimated SCU Count:** 200+ SCUs
- **Length Range:** 1,000+ m
- **Notes:** Unique engineering, custom layouts, often House or Imperial assets.

  - **Dreadnaught:** Overbuilt first-rank war frame above the ordinary capital band
  - **Titan:** City-ship or sector-flagship frame that demands true titanic hardware
  - **Industrial Complex:** Mobile factory-habitat or yard-block frame whose output matters more than maneuver grace
