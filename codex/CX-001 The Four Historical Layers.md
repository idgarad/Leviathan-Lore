# CX-001: The Four Historical Layers

Document Status: Foundational Canon
Primary Layer: Multi-Layer
Subject Domain: Civilization
Audience: Developers | Authors | Readers

## Abstract

This volume defines the four historical layers used to organize Leviathan canon: Eldritch Layer, Hyborean Era, Ancient History, and Modern Times. These layers are not merely chronological bins. They also describe how much can be known, how evidence survives, and how different kinds of truth enter the setting.

## Diegetic Record

The modern schools divide the past into four descending strata. They do this not out of vanity, but from necessity. The deeper the past, the less the past resembles a stable archive and the more it resembles pressure acting through fragment, ruin, inheritance, terror, and myth.

The highest stratum is Modern Times. Here history is a disciplined craft. Records are produced intentionally. Archives are curated. Sensor logs, bureaucratic files, testimony chains, industrial ledgers, and state or corporate memory systems allow events to be tracked with comparatively high fidelity. Fraud remains possible, but method exists to confront it.

Beneath Modern Times lies Ancient History. This is the age of documentable civilization before the formal triumph of modern historical doctrine. Its peoples kept annals, monuments, chronicles, genealogies, legal texts, and sacred or political records. Much is missing, partisan, or damaged, yet enough survives that sequence, cause, and consequence can often be reconstructed with confidence.

Beneath Ancient History lies the Hyborean Era, the lost age. It is not false. Its stones are real. Its metals are real. Its ruined vaults, roadbeds, bones, foundations, machine fragments, burial complexes, and ritual landscapes are real. Yet its self-explanations survive only in distortion. It is remembered through legend, conquest tales, prophetic fragments, cult inheritance, place taboos, and objects divorced from their makers. The Hyborean world exists in the record as matter more than testimony.

At the deepest level lies the Eldritch Layer. This stratum precedes humanity and perhaps precedes categories that human history finds comfortable. It is encountered as intrusion, contamination, geometry without human purpose, impossible longevity, non-anthropic intention, and dread that outlives explanation. Where the Hyborean Era leaves ruins, the Eldritch Layer leaves wounds in ontology. It is the oldest pressure in the setting and the least safely interpreted.

Thus the four layers are not only ages of time. They are four different relationships between truth and survivability.

## Analytical Breakdown

### Eldritch Layer

The Eldritch Layer is the pre-human depth of the setting. It carries the cosmic-horror inheritance of the franchise. Evidence from this layer is fragmentary, dangerous, and often legible only by negative effect. It is best used to create deep time, existential unease, and the sense that civilization rests on an older and indifferent substrate.

### Hyborean Era

The Hyborean Era is the mythic lost age. Physical evidence exists, but explanatory context is badly degraded. This layer is ideal for relics, dead polities, vanished technologies, heroic cycles, collapsed ecologies, and contested origin stories.

### Ancient History

Ancient History begins where a reasonably documentable chain of human or post-human civilization becomes reconstructable. This layer allows dynastic politics, migrations, early interstellar expansions, doctrinal schisms, trade regimes, and the long consequences of statecraft.

### Modern Times

Modern Times is a separate fourth layer defined by formal historical doctrine. Here the keeping of records is itself institutionalized. Investigators can compare archives, audit claims, critique bias, and produce histories that are more than court propaganda or inherited legend.

## Material Implications

The layer model governs how lore should be written. Eldritch material should remain sparse, unsettling, and consequence-rich. Hyborean material should privilege artifact, remnant, and contradictory memory. Ancient History should support robust chronologies and political causality. Modern material should withstand close scrutiny because the setting itself now contains disciplined observers capable of exposing weak claims.

This model also protects tone. The universe can contain cosmic horror, mythic grandeur, classical rise-and-fall history, and hard-edged modern geopolitics without forcing them into one undifferentiated narrative voice.

## Author Notes

Purpose: This is the primary organizational document for canon. It defines both chronology and epistemic style.

Reasoning: The setting needs room for myth, archaeology, hard history, and near-contemporary realism without collapsing them into the same evidence standard. The layer model solves that by tying tone and certainty to depth.

Math And Constraint Checks: Each layer still obeys consequences. Even poorly understood eras leave physically consistent remains, demographic effects, territorial shifts, language drift, and recoverable material signatures.

FTL Hand-Waves: None in this volume. Any FTL-enabled civilization placed in a layer must still show the logistical and historical consequences of possessing it.

Open Questions: Ancient History will eventually need a franchise-specific cutoff that marks the transition into Modern Times.
