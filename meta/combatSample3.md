# Combat Simulation Log: combatSample3.md

## Scenario: "Sledgehammer vs. Flies"

### Narrative Summary

Three ships engage in a close-quarters skirmish near the debris fields of the Outer Belt. The defending ship, *ISS Goliath*, is a heavy destroyer with formidable main batteries and thick deflector coverage. The attackers, *Valkyrie* and *Wasp*, are smaller, agile corvettes equipped with beam lances and missile racks.
Every shot fired—by any ship—generates waste heat, so careful fire discipline is essential to avoid overheating your own systems. 

The corvettes split, approaching from opposite vectors. Their goal: get under Goliath's guns, where its main batteries cannot track fast enough, and use sustained beam fire and missile salvos to overwhelm its thermal management and force a withdrawal.

*Valkyrie* executes a textbook high-transversal run, skimming Goliath's hull at close range, its beams carving glowing scars across the deflector layers. Missiles detonate in rapid succession, saturating the shield's coupling limit and dumping heat into Goliath's thermal battery. Goliath's main guns struggle to track the nimble corvette, shots going wide as Valkyrie jinks and rolls.

*Wasp*, however, misjudges its approach, cutting its velocity vector too soon. Goliath's fire control locks on, and a main battery slug punches through Wasp's deflectors, breaching the hull. With its SI dropping and thermal alarms blaring, Wasp is forced to break off and retreat, trailing vaporized armor and venting atmosphere.

With one aggressor gone, Goliath focuses its defensive fire on Valkyrie. But Valkyrie, maintaining high transversal, continues to pour on the heat. Goliath's thermal battery climbs toward critical broil. Rather than risk a catastrophic overheat, Goliath initiates a tactical withdrawal, its battered deflectors flickering as it accelerates away.

Throughout the engagement, all ships must manage not only incoming fire but also the waste heat generated by their own weapons. Firing main batteries, beams, and missiles adds to each ship's thermal load. The Valid Firing Solution (VFS) doctrine is critical: it ensures that ships only fire when a hit is likely, minimizing wasted shots and unnecessary heat buildup. In this battle, Goliath's gunners are forced to hold fire at times to avoid overheating, while the corvettes must balance aggression with thermal discipline.

### Tactical Lessons
- Large ships with slow-tracking weapons are vulnerable to small, agile opponents that maximize transversal velocity.
- Overwhelming a target's thermal management is often more effective than brute force penetration.
- Poor piloting or tactical errors (low transversal) can result in catastrophic losses for small ships.
- Firing discipline is essential: every shot generates waste heat, so VFS logic prevents wasteful shots that could overheat your own ship.
